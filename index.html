Projects at university

During my studies I worked on a couple of projects either in a team or on my own. The most notable are

  • Fall 2016 to Spring 2017: Bachelor thesis - Extension of an application for thermal analysis of satellites
    Keywords: heat equation, system of stiff nonlinear differential equations, Qt interface in Python, CVODE ODE solver
    Tasks included choosing numerical methods to efficiently solve a the heat equation with with (nonlinear) radiative heat transfer terms. The discretization typically consisted of 100 to 10000 nodes and the focus was on the efficiency of the implementation. To allow engineers to use the solver, its parameters and some post-processing features were integrated into a Qt-based interface.
  • 2014-2016: IFAS valveplate/cylinderblock project - As part of a project examining losses in hydraulic pumps at IFAS, I supported the development of an application used to analyze the behavior of a critical bearing contact. My tasks included modeling of physical effects for the simulation and implementing the mathematical backend and a Qt frontend. A paper on the project was published at IFK 2016.
  • Summer 2016: Modeling of offshore windturbine wakes - During a group project at MathCCES I examined models to predict the wake of offshore windturbines and their effect on subsequent turbines. The focus of the project was to find a model which is suitable for large scale optimiazation problems. We derived and implemented a finite volume discretization of the two-dimensional Eddy Viscosity wake model and made comparisons with an OpenFOAM model using the SOWFA toolbox.
  • 2015: TrussSimulator - As part of my degree, students have to complete an assignment in a team to develop a small application with an engineering background. The goal is to get familiar with all stages of software development including engineering, implementation, testing and documentation. Our task was to develop an easy to use tool that allows the user to construct a two-dimensional truss and to compute its deformation under load. The tool is written in C++ and uses the Qt library for the interface and SUNDIALS KINSOL as a nonlinear solver. Unfortunately we were not allowed to share our git repository due to the assignment character of the project.
